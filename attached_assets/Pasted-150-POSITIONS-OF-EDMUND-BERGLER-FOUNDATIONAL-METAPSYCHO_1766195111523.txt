150 POSITIONS OF EDMUND BERGLER
================================

FOUNDATIONAL METAPSYCHOLOGY
---------------------------
1. The pre-Oedipal oral phase (0-18 months) is more fundamental to neurosis than the Oedipal phase (3-5 years).
2. There is only one "basic neurosis"—repressed masochistic attachment to the fantasied earliest "bad mother."
3. Psychic masochism is the unconscious pursuit of "pleasure in displeasure."
4. Psychic masochism constitutes "the life-blood of neurosis" and is in fact the basic neurosis.
5. Neurotic equals psychic masochist—every neurotic is fundamentally a psychic masochist.
6. The superego is the "real master of the personality."
7. The superego operates as "torture for torture's sake."
8. The punitive part of the superego (Daimonion) uses the ego ideal to torture the ego.
9. Punishment itself becomes masochized—the neurotic derives unconscious pleasure from being punished.
10. Infantile megalomania and infantile omnipotence are of prime importance in the reduction of anxiety.
11. Man's inhumanity to man is equaled only by man's inhumanity to himself.
12. People are heavily defended against realization of the darkest aspects of human nature—their emotional addiction to unresolved negative emotions.
13. The infant's first important emotional relationship is with the mother (breast/bottle) during the oral stage.
14. Later neuroses "reformulating" oral masochistic material represent "rescue stations."
15. Neurotics cling to and repeat misery, which in itself indicates psychic masochism.

THE MECHANISM OF ORALITY
------------------------
16. The "mechanism of orality" is paradigmatic for the masochistic character.
17. The mechanism of orality consists of a three-act triad operating unconsciously.
18. Act 1: Through provocative behavior, a situation is brought about in which some substitute of the pre-Oedipal mother is "refusing."
19. Act 2: Not realizing they manufacture disappointment, neurotics become aggressive in "righteous indignation" and seemingly in self-defense.
20. Act 3: They indulge in endless self-pity, unconsciously enjoying psychic masochism.
21. Neurotics repress completely the fact of their own initial provocation that began the sequence.
22. Neurotics repress the masochistic enjoyment of self-pity.
23. The ego-strengthening illusion of "aggression" is maintained while the decisive masochistic substructure remains hidden.
24. Psychic masochism unconsciously "sugarcoats" and "neutralizes" pain.
25. Consciously, pain remains and is felt in symptoms and personality distortions.
26. Psychic masochists unconsciously want refusal, rejection, humiliation, and defeat.
27. The genuine "wish to get" of infancy is now a defense in the adult neurotic.
28. Those who operate by the mechanism of orality are "injustice collectors."
29. A person who unconsciously runs after disappointment cannot be consciously happy.
30. Neurotics cast others as the "bad, refusing mother."

FIVE-LAYER STRUCTURE OF NEUROTIC SYMPTOMS
-----------------------------------------
31. Neurotic symptoms and traits have a five-layer structure.
32. Psychic masochism is disguised from the superego by two defensive alibis.
33. In the normal person, the fifth layer is antimasochistic.
34. In neurosis, the final layer "smuggles in" masochism in self-damaging symptoms.
35. Neurotics cannot be helped unless their psychic masochism is analyzed.
36. Every clinical entity incorporates a unique "specific additional factor" on top of the basic mechanism.

THE BASIC FALLACY AND PSEUDOAGGRESSION
--------------------------------------
37. Neurotics shift the blame outside, mostly to parents—this is the "basic fallacy."
38. The basic fallacy must be shown to be a fallacy for treatment to succeed.
39. All neurotic aggression is "pseudoaggression."
40. Pseudoaggression promotes self-damage rather than genuine assertion.
41. Neurotic aggression serves masochistic purposes while appearing defensive.
42. The neurotic believes he is fighting back when in fact he is engineering his own defeat.

SUPEREGO THEORY
---------------
43. The superego requires constant appeasement.
44. Psychic masochism constitutes the most dangerous countermeasure of the unconscious ego against the superego's tyranny.
45. Inner guilt drives repetitive self-damaging behavior.
46. The "inner conscience" (superego) constantly accuses the ego.
47. The unconscious ego defends against superego reproaches through various mechanisms.
48. When superego attacks are mild ("raids"), neurotic symptoms may be transitory.
49. Neurotic symptoms have a "pseudo-moral connotation"—ironization of teachings to prop up each symptom.
50. The battle of the conscience is the central drama of neurotic life.

WRITER'S BLOCK AND CREATIVITY
-----------------------------
51. Writer's block is a clinical condition rooted in oral masochism.
52. Writer's block was coined as a term in 1947.
53. Writers are fortunate in that they are able to treat their neurosis every day by writing.
54. As soon as the writer is blocked, this is catastrophic because the writer will start to go to pieces.
55. Many a person cannot bear the thought of being completely controlled by the unconscious.
56. The productive writer eliminates, intrapsychically, the mother—autarchically giving himself ideas and words (milk).
57. The successful writer sets a "magic gesture" into motion—dramatizing how he allegedly wanted to be treated.
58. The unproductive, neurotically inhibited writer exhausts his psychic energy in the creation of his unconscious alibi of pseudo-aggression ("I refuse").
59. The inhibited writer retains the unconscious masochistic wish to be refused.
60. When the writer cannot convince his superego of the "harmlessness" of his imagination, he has no ideas at all.
61. If the writer clears the imagination hurdle but cannot obtain approval of his exhibitionism in "writing it down," he cannot write.
62. Creative writing involves unconscious autarchic provision of maternal nurturance to oneself.

GAMBLING PSYCHOLOGY
-------------------
63. All gamblers gamble because of psychic masochism.
64. The gambler has an unconscious wish to lose.
65. Losing is necessary to establish the cruelty of fate for the gambler.
66. Gambling involves the re-creation of the infantile conflict with the refusing mother.
67. The mysterious thrill in gambling is connected to unconscious masochistic enjoyment.
68. Gamblers experience a feeling of uncanniness connected to omnipotence fantasies.
69. There is inner identity between games of "chance" and games of "reasoning."
70. Speculators (big and small) operate on similar psychological dynamics as other gamblers.
71. There exists a typology of gamblers based on their specific masochistic configurations.
72. Pathological gambling shows clinical complexity involving multiple neurotic layers.
73. Superstition and systems in gambling serve defensive functions.
74. Gambling is objectively a losing proposition that satisfies masochistic needs.

MARRIAGE, DIVORCE, AND SEXUALITY
--------------------------------
75. Divorce is only a neurotic solution applied to neurotic persons.
76. Changing the external factors of one's marriage cannot alter the inner conflict.
77. Unhappy marriages result from neurotic mate selection based on the mechanism of orality.
78. Neurotic marriages involve partners unconsciously seeking to repeat infantile conflicts.
79. Tender love has unconscious connotations that are completely disregarded by purely biological approaches.
80. Frigidity in women is a manifestation of oral masochistic regression.
81. Psychic impotence in men is rooted in pre-Oedipal conflicts.
82. Premature ejaculation is the most frequent form of psychogenic potency disturbance.
83. Vaginal orgasm exists contrary to Kinsey's denial.
84. "Counterfeit-sex" describes sexual behaviors that are neurotic substitutes for genuine sexuality.
85. Neurotic women often choose termagants (difficult partners) to repeat masochistic patterns.
86. The "allure of the forbidden" operates in neurotic sexual patterns.
87. Romantic love and sexual love have distinct psychoanalytic meanings.
88. Monogamy and its difficulties reflect underlying oral conflicts.

MIDDLE-AGE REVOLT
-----------------
89. The revolt of the middle-aged man is a specific clinical syndrome.
90. Middle-age rebellion originates in unresolved oral conflicts reactivated at midlife.
91. The middle-aged rebel unconsciously seeks to recreate infantile disappointments.
92. The rebel's wife plays a role in the masochistic drama.
93. Children serve as judges in the middle-age revolt scenario.
94. Resignation is not the way out of the middle-age crisis.
95. The "second adolescence" of middle age is a defensive configuration.
96. Depressed mood in middle age relates to masochistic patterns.
97. The ménage à trois fantasy often emerges in middle-age revolt.

INJUSTICE COLLECTORS
--------------------
98. Injustice collectors are unconscious masochists who nurse resentments.
99. Injustice collectors unconsciously provoke the very mistreatment they complain about.
100. The injustice collector maintains an enormous cache of grievances going back years.
101. Injustice collectors unconsciously enjoy self-pity.
102. Injustice collectors believe they want normal pleasure but run after disappointment.
103. The injustice collector's versions of events are often skewed or completely misinterpreted.
104. Injustice collecting serves to validate the masochistic attachment to the bad mother.

SPECIFIC NEUROTIC CONDITIONS
----------------------------
105. Boredom ("alysosis") is a neurotic disease, not merely a mood state.
106. Boredom is closely associated in a triad with scopophilia (voyeurism) and psychic masochism.
107. Boredom represents an unsuccessful defense mechanism against inner conscience reproaches.
108. The prognosis is unfavorable in untreated boredom, which may result in suicide.
109. Boredom is progressive and never self-limited.
110. Erythrophobia (pathological blushing) is a manifestation of oral regression.
111. Logorrhoea (compulsive talking) serves defensive functions against masochistic guilt.
112. Kleptomania involves the mechanism of orality.
113. The impostor syndrome has oral masochistic roots.
114. Hypocrisy has specific implications in neurosis and criminal psychopathology.
115. Cynicism is a mechanism deducible from oral masochistic theory.
116. "Criminosis" describes criminal behavior rooted in masochistic self-damage.
117. There exist 22 distinct visual neuroses.
118. Psychogenic aspermia (inability to ejaculate) has oral masochistic origins.
119. Alcoholism is treatable as an oral regression condition.
120. Hypochondria serves masochistic defensive purposes.

FASHION AND CULTURE
-------------------
121. Fashion involves unconscious motivations related to fear and defense.
122. Clothes are a masculine invention despite being associated with women.
123. Fashion contains paradoxes and contradictions of the "improved skin."
124. There are seven ingredients of haute couture related to unconscious dynamics.
125. Dresses relate to three types of reputation.
126. Clothes, children of man's fear, become independent cultural phenomena.
127. Some women have "sartorial anti-talents" based on unconscious conflicts.

HUMOR AND LAUGHTER
------------------
128. Laughter and the sense of humor have specific psychoanalytic meanings.
129. Humor involves complex dynamics of aggression and masochism.
130. The psychology of humor relates to superego dynamics.

CLINICAL TECHNIQUE
------------------
131. Therapists should talk at length to patients to counteract their projection of "bad refusing mother."
132. More active analyzing is needed to unearth and interpret all repressed masochistic data.
133. Repetitions must be identified and interpreted systematically.
134. The transference relationship involves projection of the bad mother onto the analyst.
135. Working through requires addressing the basic masochistic attachment.
136. Neurotics who want to change can be cured; those who don't remain "incurable."
137. There are curable and incurable neurotics based on their willingness to analyze psychic masochism.
138. "Malignant" psychic masochism differs from ordinary neurotic masochism.

PARENTS AND CHILDREN
--------------------
139. Parents are not guilty of their children's neuroses in the simple causal way commonly believed.
140. The child's subjective interpretation of maternal behavior creates the "bad mother" imago.
141. The fantasied bad mother may differ substantially from the actual mother's behavior.
142. Children's neuroses involve the same oral masochistic dynamics as adult neuroses.

CRITIQUE OF KINSEY
------------------
143. Kinsey's research avoids with 100 percent completeness any concession to the existence of the dynamic unconscious.
144. The Kinsey scale is based on flawed assumptions.
145. Kinsey's biological one-sidedness ignores psychodynamic factors.
146. The "taxonomic approach" fails to recognize the unconscious dimension of human sexuality.
147. Kinsey's dismissal of premature ejaculation as a disorder is unjustified.
148. Early puberty is an antedated defense expressed organically, not merely biological.

GENERAL THEORETICAL POSITIONS
-----------------------------
149. Derogatory remarks about Freudian psychoanalysis are mainly based on ignorance or resistance, or both.
150. The oral phase is the most determining factor in the development of multiple psychological conditions, extending Freud's later insights about pre-Oedipal development.
